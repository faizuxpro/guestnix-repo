# Airbnb House Manual Template (Free): Compliance-Ready, Guest-Readable, and Easy to Maintain

An Airbnb house manual should do three jobs at once: prevent avoidable guest errors, reduce host support load, and provide documented operational guidance when incidents occur. A strong manual is structured, testable, and version-controlled. If your manual is not maintained like an operations document, it will fail in real-world stays.

## Quick Answer
Use a modular house manual with:
- Required safety and access sections.
- Property-specific operating instructions.
- Policy language that is clear and enforceable.
- Monthly update cadence and ownership.

## Important Note
This is an operations template, not legal advice.  
Adapt rules to your local regulations, platform policies, lease constraints, and insurance requirements.

## Master House Manual Template (Operational Format)

```text
Document Title: [Property Name] House Manual
Version: [vX.Y]
Last Updated: [YYYY-MM-DD]
Owner: [Name/Role]

A. FAST START (Guest first 5 minutes)
1) Address and entry map
2) Access method + backup access path
3) Wi-Fi network + password
4) Urgent support channel

B. PROPERTY RULES (Boundary clarity)
1) Occupancy limits
2) Quiet hours
3) Visitor policy
4) Smoking/vaping policy
5) Pet policy
6) Event/party policy

C. HOME SYSTEMS (Error prevention)
1) Thermostat and HVAC controls
2) Water heater/hot water notes
3) Kitchen appliances
4) Laundry and drying
5) Waste sorting and bin day

D. SAFETY AND EMERGENCY
1) Emergency numbers
2) Nearest hospital/clinic
3) Fire safety equipment locations
4) Water/gas/electric shutoff basics
5) Incident reporting process

E. LOCAL ESSENTIALS
1) Grocery
2) Pharmacy
3) Medical urgent care
4) Transport options

F. CHECKOUT
1) Checkout time
2) Required guest actions
3) Lock-up steps
4) Forgotten-items process
```

## Risk Matrix You Should Include
| Risk type | Likelihood | Impact | Preventive content | Response owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lockout | Medium | High | Entry + backup access section | Frontline host |
| Utilities interruption | Low-Med | High | Utility fallback steps | Ops lead |
| Noise complaint | Medium | Medium | Rules + warning policy | Host/co-host |
| Appliance misuse | Medium | Medium | Step-by-step appliance guide | Content owner |
| Safety incident | Low | Critical | Emergency and escalation protocol | Host + emergency services |

Use this matrix to prioritize section quality and update frequency.

## Policy Wording Library (Editable)

## Quiet Hours Policy
```text
Quiet hours are from [start time] to [end time].
Please keep noise at conversational level during this period.
Repeated violations may result in booking termination per listing policy.
```

## Visitor Policy
```text
Only registered guests are allowed on the property unless pre-approved in writing.
Unapproved visitors may trigger additional fees or reservation cancellation.
```

## Smoking Policy
```text
Smoking/vaping is [not allowed/allowed only in designated area].
Violation incurs a remediation fee of [amount] due to cleaning and odor treatment requirements.
```

## Appliance Instruction Pattern (That Reduces Support Tickets)
For each appliance, use:
- Purpose.
- 3-step normal use.
- Most common failure and fix.
- When to escalate.

Example card:

```text
Appliance: Dishwasher
Normal use:
1) Load with spray arms unobstructed
2) Add one pod in dispenser
3) Select Normal cycle + Start
If issue:
- Water not draining: check filter basket first
Escalate:
- If standing water remains after reset cycle
```

## Emergency Micro-Playbooks
Keep response instructions concise:

## Fire Alarm Triggered
1. Exit property immediately.
2. Call emergency number.
3. Notify host only after safe evacuation.

## Water Leak
1. Turn off local valve if safe.
2. Move electronics away from water.
3. Message host with photo and location.

## Power Outage
1. Check building-wide status.
2. Use flashlight location listed in manual.
3. Contact host if outage exceeds threshold.

## Checkout Protocol (Host and Guest Alignment)
Guest checklist should be short.  
Host verification checklist should be separate and internal.

Guest-facing:
- Lock doors and windows.
- Dispose trash as instructed.
- Message host at departure.

Host-facing internal checklist:
- Timestamped photo set.
- Access device reset check.
- Linen turnover verification.
- Damage notation workflow.

## Version Control and Governance
Treat manual changes like product updates:
- `Major`: policy or safety changes.
- `Minor`: operational clarification.
- `Patch`: typo or formatting fix.

Track:
- Change date.
- Owner.
- Reason.
- Sections modified.

## Audit Procedure (Monthly)
1. Review top recurring guest questions.
2. Check incident log for avoidable issues.
3. Update high-friction sections first.
4. Re-test critical instructions from a guest phone.
5. Publish updated version and archive old version.

## FAQ

## Is a house manual different from a welcome book?
Yes. The manual is operational and policy-heavy; the welcome book is orientation and stay experience focused.

## Should I include penalties in the manual?
Yes, where appropriate and policy-compliant, but keep wording professional and specific.

## How do I avoid legal risk in manual language?
Use clear, factual instructions and align with local laws and platform rules. Avoid ambiguous threats.

## Can one template work for all properties?
Use a core template, then add property-specific overrides for access, systems, and safety details.

## What causes the most manual failures?
Outdated access details, vague policy wording, and missing fallback instructions.
